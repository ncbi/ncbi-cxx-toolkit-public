Using CVS on MS Windows in NCBI

  1. Download the CVS executable (you also can find it at http://www.cyclic.com/) to C:\WINNT\System32\cvs.exe.
  2. Create environment variable CVSROOT:
    • Click the right mouse button on the icon of your NT PC name (it is usually located in the upper left corner of NT desktop), and select item "Properties".
    • Form "System Properties shows up. -- Here, choose folder "Environment" and click on the lower window ("User Variables for Yourname:") on the string with TEMP variable.
    • Now, type "CVSROOT" in the "Variable:" text field, then type something like :server:yourlogname@ray:/src/NCBI/vault.ncbi in the "Value:" text field. Here, the yourlogname stands for your NCBI account name with all letter lowercased. :server:beloslyu@ray:/src/NCBI/vault.ncbi
    • Press the "Set" button. The new variable CVSROOT and its value should appear in the lower window("User Variables for Yourname:").
    • Press the "Apply" button.
    • Press the "OK" button.
    • Logout, then login to your PC again.
  3. Login to machine "ray" and add the following two lines to your ~/.rhosts file:
    • pcname username (for example: dnb beloslyu)
    • pcname Username (for example: dnb Beloslyu)
    where the pcname stands for your PC name, username is your NCBI UNIX domain account name(usually all lowercased), and Username is your NCBI NT domain account name(usually it is just like the UNIX domain account name, but with the first letter uppercased).
